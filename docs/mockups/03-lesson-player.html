History of AI Development

7 min read · Category: AI Fundamentals

Artificial Intelligence has a history spanning over 70 years, from its conceptual origins in the 1950s to the deep learning revolution of the 2020s. Understanding this history is essential for the IPAS exam, as questions frequently test your knowledge of key milestones and their significance.

The Birth of AI (1950s)

In 1950, Alan Turing published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the famous Turing Test. The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined at the Dartmouth Conference in 1956, organized by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon.

Key Concept

The Turing Test evaluates a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from a human. This concept appears frequently on the IPAS beginner exam.

AI Winter & Revival (1970s-1990s)

After initial excitement, AI research experienced two "AI Winters" — periods of reduced funding and interest due to unmet expectations. The first winter occurred in the 1970s after the Lighthill Report criticized AI research progress. The second winter came in the late 1980s when expert systems failed to meet commercial expectations.

Revival came in the 1990s with advances in machine learning algorithms and increasing computational power. IBM's Deep Blue defeating world chess champion Garry Kasparov in 1997 marked a pivotal moment.

The Deep Learning Era (2010s-Present)

The current era began with the ImageNet breakthrough in 2012, when deep neural networks dramatically outperformed traditional methods in image recognition. Key developments include:

  • AlphaGo defeating Go champion Lee Sedol (2016)
  • Transformer architecture and attention mechanism (2017)
  • GPT and large language models (2018-present)
  • Generative AI and multimodal models (2023-present)
Exam Tip

The IPAS exam often asks about the sequence of AI milestones. Remember: Turing Test (1950) -> Dartmouth (1956) -> AI Winters (1970s, 1980s) -> Deep Blue (1997) -> ImageNet (2012) -> AlphaGo (2016) -> Transformers (2017).
