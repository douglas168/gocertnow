# University Club Outreach Playbook

**Purpose:** Land 2–3 bulk-deal partnerships with Taiwanese university AI/CS/business clubs by Week 10.
**Economics:** NT$1,200 × 20 seats = NT$24,000 per club deal. 3 clubs = NT$72,000 + 60 paying students in one motion.
**Timing:** Run this campaign in Phase 2 (Weeks 7–10, after June IPAS exam pass data exists).

---

## Why this works

1. **Club presidents are looking for "value deliverables" to their members** — every club needs to justify its existence with concrete benefits to members. Your NT$1,200/seat IPAS AI course is a free win for them to advertise.
2. **Students follow their club's recommendations** — peer influence is strong in Taiwanese campus culture. One endorsed course in a club = 20–40 paying students with ~0 CAC.
3. **Institutional signal** — once you can say *"台大 AI Club 指定教材"*, your landing page credibility jumps massively for future cold traffic.
4. **Future pipeline** — today's club members are next year's career starters who will want AWS / PMP / AI certs. You're buying loyalty cheap.

## If a club says yes

### Step 1: Discovery call (15 min video or LINE call)
Questions to ask the president:
- 社團現在有多少活躍社員？
- 社員中大概有多少對 AI 證照有興趣？
- 社團有沒有其他付費課程的合作經驗？
- 希望什麼時候開始？
- 偏好收費流程是社團代收還是社員個別付款？

### Step 2: Send partnership one-pager (NOT a legal contract)
Keep it informal for v1. A Google Doc with:
- Pricing confirmation: NT$1,200/seat, minimum 20 seats
- Timeline: open signup for 2 weeks, close cohort, kick off
- What LevelCert provides (list 4 items from email)
- What the club provides (1 joint announcement, attendance at the kickoff Q&A, help collecting testimonials)
- Social post snippets they can reuse

### Step 3: Joint launch announcement
Co-create the announcement with the club president. Post from:
- Club's FB page / IG
- Your own Threads / IG
- Your own LINE official account

### Step 4: Kickoff Q&A session (30 min, Google Meet)
- 10 min: founder intro + why you built LevelCert
- 10 min: walkthrough of the platform
- 10 min: open Q&A on iPAS exam specifically

### Step 5: Track results
- Log each student's name + signup date
- Ping each student 1 week before their exam
- Collect pass/fail result within 3 days of each exam
- Ask top 3 scorers for testimonials

## Campaign targets

- **Emails sent:** 10 (all 10 target clubs)
- **Reply rate target:** 3 clubs reply (30%)
- **Discovery calls:** 2 clubs accept a call
- **Closed deals:** 1 confirmed bulk deal (10% conversion)
- **Timeline:** Weeks 7–10 of roadmap (June 2026)

If you close even 1 club deal at 20 students, that's NT$24,000 + 20 new students + a campus credibility anchor. That's a better outcome than 100 hours of paid social ads.

---

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't send this as a form letter. Spend 10 minutes per club personalizing the opening.
- ❌ Don't contact more than 10 clubs at once. You won't be able to handle the follow-up.
- ❌ Don't offer lower than NT$1,200/seat, even if they push. Your floor is the floor. Discounts damage positioning.
- ❌ Don't negotiate minimum seats below 20. The deal stops being worth it for you.
- ❌ Don't promise things you can't deliver (custom content, TA support, graded assignments).
- ❌ Don't talk down about competing institutional courses. Clubs may have existing relationships — stay respectful.
