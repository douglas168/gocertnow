# "Act like a growth strategist. Build a plan to reach 1M people in 30 days for:  levelcert.com

List top 5 acquisition channels

Define content formats for each

Suggest daily execution plan

Include organic + paid mix

Focus on leverage, not efforts

Output as step-by-step system."

Below is a **30-day reach system** for **LevelCert.com** built to maximize exposure, not just effort. The fastest path to 1M people is to combine **short-form video reach, creator/partner distribution, Reddit/community seeding, paid amplification, and one high-intent email/landing funnel**.[^1][^2][^3][^4]

## Reach goal

Your objective is not 1M unique buyers in 30 days. It is **1M qualified impressions/views** across the right channels, then convert a small slice into email leads, waitlist signups, or trial users. For an education product, the leverage comes from reaching people where educational clips and career advice already travel: YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok/Reels/Shorts, Reddit, and paid social retargeting.[^5][^6][^7][^4][^1]

## Top 5 channels

1. **YouTube Shorts + long-form YouTube**
Best overall for educational reach, discoverability, and trust; one short can hook, and one long video can convert.[^6][^3][^1][^5]
2. **TikTok / Instagram Reels**
Best for fast organic testing and viral hooks; short-form video is still the highest-leverage reach format for top-of-funnel discovery.[^8][^9][^1]
3. **LinkedIn**
Best for 20–35-year-old professionals because the product is career-oriented and certification-driven; it also supports authority content and founder-led posts.[^2][^10][^4]
4. **Reddit + niche communities**
Best for credibility and feedback loops; Reddit conversations influence search and AI recommendation surfaces more than polished brand posts do.[^11][^7]
5. **Paid social retargeting**
Best for turning attention into action; use Meta and YouTube paid to retarget viewers, site visitors, and engaged social users rather than buying cold traffic first.[^12][^13][^4][^2]

## Channel formats

### 1) YouTube

Use three content formats:

- **Shorts**: 20–45 seconds, one exam tip, one mistake, one myth, one answer.
- **8–12 minute breakdowns**: “How to pass X cert,” “What top candidates do differently.”
- **Live/mock exam walkthroughs**: screen-share, exam-style reasoning, and Q\&A.[^3][^1][^5]


### 2) TikTok / Reels

Use:

- **Hook-first micro tips**: “If you’re studying AI certs, stop doing this.”
- **Series format**: “30 days to certification.”
- **Side-by-side comparison clips**: bad vs good study method, passive vs active recall.[^9][^1][^8]


### 3) LinkedIn

Use:

- **Founder posts**: opinionated, career-focused, direct.
- **Carousel docs**: frameworks, checklists, study systems.
- **Mini case studies**: “How a learner went from zero to readiness.”
- **Polls**: certification pain points, time available, study habits.[^10][^4][^2]


### 4) Reddit

Use:

- **Problem-first posts** in relevant subreddits.
- **Comment-driven value**: answer questions before linking anything.
- **Comparison threads**: “How do I study for X cert faster?”
- **AMA-style posts** from the founder or early users.[^7][^11]


### 5) Paid social

Use:

- **Meta video retargeting** for viewers and site visitors.
- **YouTube retargeting** for viewers of Shorts/long-form.
- **LinkedIn Sponsored Content** only for narrow B2B or professional pathways.
- **Lead ads** only after you have proof of hook and landing page conversion.[^4][^12][^2]


## Content engine

Your content should be built from one idea factory:

- **Pain point clips**: “You’re studying the wrong way.”
- **Mistake clips**: “Why most certification prep fails.”
- **Framework clips**: “The 3-step study loop.”
- **Proof clips**: mock exam walkthroughs, score improvements, before/after readiness.
- **Founder POV clips**: why LevelCert exists and who it helps.

One idea should become:

- 1 YouTube Short.
- 1 TikTok/Reel.
- 1 LinkedIn post.
- 1 Reddit comment or post.
- 1 email/waitlist snippet.
- 1 landing page block.[^1][^5][^3]


## Daily execution plan

### Daily production rhythm

- **1 long-form asset** or recorded live session.
- **3–5 short clips** cut from that asset.
- **1 LinkedIn post**.
- **3–5 Reddit comments/posts**.
- **1 email or waitlist touch**.
- **1 paid ad iteration or creative test review**.[^12][^4][^1]


### Daily workflow

1. Morning: pick one pain point.
2. Record one anchor video.
3. Cut into multiple shorts.
4. Post to Shorts, TikTok, Reels.
5. Post one LinkedIn insight.
6. Seed one Reddit discussion.
7. Review analytics in the evening.
8. Reuse best-performing hook the next day.

## 30-day system

### Days 1–3: Set the machine

- Build 3 landing pages: AI cert prep, study system, mock exam promise.
- Set up tracking pixels, retargeting, and UTM structure.
- Draft 30 hooks, 30 titles, 30 post ideas.
- Make 10 short clips before launch.
- Define one clear lead magnet: “AI Certification Readiness Checklist.”


### Days 4–10: Test hooks

- Publish 2–3 Shorts/Reels per day.
- Publish 1 LinkedIn post per day.
- Publish 1 Reddit post every 2 days.
- Launch small paid tests on 3–5 best creatives.
- Kill weak hooks fast.


### Days 11–20: Double down

- Keep only top-performing hooks.
- Convert best post into a longer YouTube video.
- Turn comments into new content.
- Retarget all video viewers with a simple CTA.
- Start a weekly live workshop or mock-exam event.


### Days 21–30: Scale winners

- Put budget behind the 2–3 best creatives.
- Expand to adjacent cert topics.
- Launch creator/partner reposts.
- Repurpose best-performing content into comparison posts and case studies.
- Drive all traffic to one conversion action: waitlist, free diagnostic, or trial.


## Organic + paid mix

A good 30-day mix is:

- **70% organic**
- **30% paid**


### Organic

- Shorts, Reels, TikTok
- LinkedIn founder content
- Reddit/community participation
- YouTube long-form and live
- Email nurturing.[^3][^7][^1]


### Paid

- Retarget video viewers.
- Retarget site visitors.
- Boost proven clips.
- Run low-budget tests on 3 audiences:
    - career switchers,
    - young professionals in tech,
    - AI certification candidates.[^2][^4][^12]


## Leverage rules

Focus on these leverage multipliers:

- **One recording → many assets**.
- **One hook → many platforms**.
- **One winner → paid amplification**.
- **One landing page → one CTA**.
- **One audience promise → repeated in every format**.

Do not:

- Make unique content for every platform.
- Overbuild the website before validating hooks.
- Spend heavy on cold ads before a message wins.
- Chase vanity reach without a conversion path.[^4][^1][^12]


## Step-by-step system

1. Define one core promise: “Pass AI certs faster with a game-like study system.”
2. Make one weekly anchor video.
3. Slice it into 3–5 short clips.
4. Publish daily on Shorts, Reels, TikTok.
5. Post daily on LinkedIn.
6. Seed weekly Reddit threads.
7. Retarget engaged users with ads.
8. Funnel everyone to a single lead magnet.
9. Reuse the highest-performing creative.
10. Repeat for 30 days.

## Expected outcome

If executed tightly, the best path to 1M people is not one viral post; it is a **content system plus paid amplification** that compounds reach across multiple platforms. For LevelCert, the strongest message is not “another course,” but **“a better way to actually pass.”**[^5][^6][^1][^4]
