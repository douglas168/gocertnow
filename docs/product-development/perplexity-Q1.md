## Best Model for Taiwan-Only, Single-Cert Vertical, <1,000 Students/Year

Given your context (you're building **levelcert.com** for **IPAS AI Planning Certification** prep, targeting Taiwan, <1,000 students/year, AI/SaaS entrepreneur):

### ❌ Avoid These Models
- **Marketplace (Udemy)**: Too much competition, race-to-bottom pricing, instructor revenue share dropping to 15% by 2026 [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dhawals_edtech-onlinelearning-creatoreconomy-activity-7297732863834173441-0AA-)
- **Ad-Supported Free (Duolingo free tier)**: Requires millions of users to be viable; ARPU too low (~$0.64/yr) [gurufocus](https://www.gurufocus.com/news/2829647/duolingo-the-owl-expands-its-wings-beyond-language?r=4bf001661e6fdd88d0cd7a5659ff9748)
- **Nonprofit/Donation (Khan Academy)**: Not scalable for a for-profit SaaS; requires massive brand/trust [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khan_Academy)

### ✅ Recommended: **Hybrid Freemium + Certification Fee + B2B Group Sales**

| Component | Rationale | Estimated Economics |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Free intro course + paid full prep** | Build trust, capture leads; upgrade prompt at natural moment  [thinkific](https://www.thinkific.com/blog/monetize-online-course/) | Free: 1,000 signups → 10–20% convert to paid |
| **Cert exam included in course price** | iPAS初级/中级 course + exam bundle = NT$8,000–$13,000 (~$260–$420)  [taiago](https://taiago.com/ipas%E5%9C%98%E5%A0%B1-%E7%B7%9A%E4%B8%8A%E8%AA%B2%E7%A8%8B-ai%E6%87%89%E7%94%A8%E8%A6%8F%E5%8A%83%E5%B8%AB/) | ARPU: ~NT$10,000 (~$320); 500 students = NT$5M (~$160K) revenue |
| **Group/Corporate discounts** | Target Taiwan SMEs for employee upskilling; 104 uses NT$10K/yr for 50 employees  [money.udn](https://money.udn.com/money/story/5612/9308319) | 20 companies × 25 employees × NT$400 = NT$200K |
| **Email nurture → repeat certs** | IPAS has初级/中级/高级; upsell advanced cert + renewals | LTV 2–3× if multiple certs |
| **No ads, no subscription** | Low volume makes ads/subscriptions unviable; focus on high-ticket cert outcome | Gross margin 80–90% (digital course) |

This mirrors **Coursera's consumer cert model** but on a micro-scale: pay-per-cert (not subscription), with free discovery content to drive conversions. [enacton](https://www.enacton.com/blog/coursera-business-model/)
