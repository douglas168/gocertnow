# Founder Photo Shoot — Week 1, Issue #4

**Purpose:** Produce one (or two) real photos of the founder for the landing page founder-story block at `web/app/(marketing)/page.tsx`. This replaces the fake testimonials and establishes the "real working professional" credibility the whole wedge depends on.

**Total time budget:** 15 minutes from walking into the location to having an edited file ready to upload. Do NOT spend longer. The point is "real and shipped," not "polished and perfect."

**Hard deadline:** Before the landing page goes live to LINE traffic. In practice: Sun Apr 12 – Mon Apr 13.

---

## The one sentence that defines the shot

> **A real working professional who just looked up from their laptop — not a teacher, not an expert, not a CEO.**

If the photo can honestly be captioned *"me, last Tuesday, studying after work,"* you nailed it. If it looks like a LinkedIn headshot or a 補習班 brochure, you missed.

---

## Absolute DO-NOTs (do not negotiate these)

- ❌ No AI generation (Gemini, Midjourney, DALL·E, Aragon, HeadshotPro, Secta, etc.) — any category
- ❌ No AI img2img "enhancement" of a real photo — regenerates the face, same problem
- ❌ No AI style transfer (Pixar, anime, watercolor, sketch)
- ❌ No stock-looking uniform (blue hoodie with studio gray background = dead giveaway)
- ❌ No suit, no tie, no lab coat, no blazer
- ❌ No professional studio setup
- ❌ No heavy retouching or face smoothing
- ❌ No big posed grin, no arms-crossed "founder pose"
- ❌ No direct flash, no ring light
- ❌ No direct harsh sunlight on face
- ❌ Do not appear in the photo holding a coffee cup with a visible brand logo

**Rule of thumb:** If it looks like marketing, you're doing it wrong. If it looks like the profile photo a friend would use on LINE, you're doing it right.

---

## Step 1 — Scout the location (5 min)

You need **one of these two shots**. Pick one before you shoot, don't try to do both in the same location.

### Option A — Clean headshot (easier, mandatory)
A spot with:
- [ ] A window providing **soft indirect daylight** (north-facing is perfect; any window NOT in direct sun works)
- [ ] A **plain, uncluttered wall** opposite the window — white, off-white, light gray, or muted neutral
- [ ] Enough space to stand ~1.5 m from the wall, with the window to your side or slightly in front
- [ ] Quiet enough that you can take 10 shots without an audience

**Candidate locations:** your apartment living room wall, a home office corner, a quiet café with a large window and plain wall, a coworking space lounge, a library alcove.

### Option B — "Caught mid-study" environment shot (optional, stronger)
Your **actual study spot**:
- [ ] Desk with laptop open (ideally to a LevelCert lesson or IPAS material)
- [ ] Notebook + pen visible
- [ ] Coffee/tea is fine (no branded cups)
- [ ] Soft light from a nearby window or a warm desk lamp
- [ ] Background slightly visible but not cluttered (books, a plant, a wall — not laundry or cables)

If you have 5 extra minutes, take both. Option A goes in the founder-story block. Option B goes in the hero or "about" section. If you only have time for one: **Option A is mandatory, Option B is a bonus.**

### Location acceptance checklist
Walk into the spot and check:
- [ ] Is there daylight from a window? (No = move to another spot)
- [ ] Is the wall behind me plain and uncluttered? (Or can I clean it up in 30 seconds?)
- [ ] Is the light on my face soft, not harsh? (Stand in the spot, hold phone camera up to your face, check — if there are hard shadows across your nose, turn 45° until they soften)
- [ ] Can I prop my phone at head height? (Desk + stack of books, windowsill, shelf, tripod if you have one)

If 3 out of 4 are yes → shoot here. Don't keep scouting.

---

## Step 2 — Technical setup (2 min)

- [ ] **Phone:** use your own phone, rear camera (better sensor than front/selfie camera)
- [ ] **Mode:** Portrait mode is OK on iPhone if subtle; otherwise just regular Photo mode
- [ ] **Resolution:** default, don't mess with settings
- [ ] **Height:** prop the phone at **your eye level** (not chest, not chin) — below eye level makes you look down on the viewer, above makes you look submissive
- [ ] **Distance:** about **1.5 m** from you — close enough to frame head + shoulders, far enough that the lens doesn't distort your face
- [ ] **Timer:** 10-second self-timer, OR burst mode if you have a remote
- [ ] **Window position:** window to your **front or 45° front-side**, never directly behind you (silhouettes you) and never directly behind the phone (blinds the camera)

---

## Step 3 — Styling (2 min)

**Wear what you actually wear.** This is the most important styling note.

### ✅ Good
- Plain t-shirt (solid color, no logos)
- Plain polo
- Casual button-down (rolled sleeves OK)
- Knit pullover
- Anything you'd wear to dinner with friends
- **Your real glasses if you wear glasses daily** — do not remove them for the photo; they're part of your actual face

### ❌ Bad
- Suit / tie / blazer
- Lab coat / uniform
- Clothing with large logos or brand names
- Anything you bought specifically for the photo
- "Business casual founder uniform" (plain hoodie with zipper, stock-looking)

### Quick grooming
- [ ] Brush hair (not style it — brush it)
- [ ] No shiny forehead: pat face with a tissue
- [ ] Check teeth if you're going to smile
- [ ] Straighten collar / shirt

**Total grooming time: under 60 seconds.** Do not style yourself into something you aren't. The audience can smell it.

---

## Step 4 — The shot list (5 min)

Take **12 shots total**. Vary slightly between shots. You will use one.

| Shot | Pose | Expression |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Straight-on, shoulders square | Neutral, no smile |
| 2 | Straight-on, shoulders square | Soft closed-mouth smile |
| 3 | Straight-on, shoulders square | Small open-mouth smile |
| 4 | Shoulders slightly turned 15° left | Neutral |
| 5 | Shoulders slightly turned 15° left | Soft smile |
| 6 | Shoulders slightly turned 15° right | Neutral |
| 7 | Shoulders slightly turned 15° right | Soft smile |
| 8 | Straight-on | Look slightly away, then back to camera at the moment of shutter |
| 9 | Straight-on | Half-smile, like you just remembered something funny |
| 10 | Straight-on | Look up as if from a laptop (for Option B) |
| 11 | Free shot — whatever feels natural | — |
| 12 | Free shot — whatever feels natural | — |

### Expression coaching (say these in your head before each shot)

- **Do NOT say "cheese"** — produces a stiff grin
- **Do say:** *"A friend just asked what I'm working on"* — produces a natural half-smile + slight eye engagement. This is the winning expression.
- **Alternative:** *"I just finished a tough lesson"* — produces relaxed, slightly tired-but-satisfied face. Good for Option B.

### Posture coaching

- Shoulders back but not military-stiff
- Chin slightly down (NOT up — up makes you look arrogant)
- Eyes looking at the lens, not the screen
- Exhale slowly before each shot — resets your face

---

## Step 5 — Selection (2 min)

Walk away from the phone for 30 seconds. Come back. Swipe through all 12 shots.

### Selection criteria (in priority order)

1. **Eyes look at the lens** (not down, not past it) — reject any shot where this is off
2. **Face looks like you normally look** — reject any shot where you look "posed"
3. **No weird mouth** — reject half-blinks, awkward lip positions, teeth-only smiles
4. **Soft even light on face** — reject shots with hard shadows across nose/under eyes
5. **Background not distracting** — can be fixed in post if everything else is good

Pick the **least-weird** shot, not the most-polished. The winner is usually the one where you think *"eh, that looks like me on a Tuesday"* — not the one that looks glamorous.

**If none of the 12 feel right:** take 6 more. Do NOT take more than 18 total. Analysis paralysis is the enemy.

---

## Step 6 — Post-production (3 min)

Open the chosen photo in **Apple Photos → Edit**.

### Allowed edits
- [ ] **Crop** to head + shoulders. Rule of thirds: eyes on the upper third line.
- [ ] **Straighten** if the horizon is tilted
- [ ] **Exposure** +/- 0.3 if the face is too dark or too bright
- [ ] **White Balance** adjustment if the window light made you too warm (orange) or too cool (blue)
- [ ] **Clean Up** (iOS 18+) to remove distracting background objects — cables, laundry, random bright spots. DO NOT use Clean Up on your face or clothing.
- [ ] **Slight shadow lift** if one side of face is too dark

### Forbidden edits
- [ ] ❌ Face smoothing / skin retouching
- [ ] ❌ Enlarge eyes / slim face / any "beauty" filters
- [ ] ❌ Replace background with a generated one
- [ ] ❌ Style filters (vintage, B&W, warm/cool presets)
- [ ] ❌ Any AI "enhance" button
- [ ] ❌ Any img2img tool

**Aim for:** the photo looks like a clean version of what the camera captured, not a different photo.

---

## Step 7 — Export + upload

- [ ] Export as **JPG**, quality 90%, max dimension **1600 px** on the long side
- [ ] Rename to `founder-portrait.jpg` (and `founder-study.jpg` if you took Option B)
- [ ] Put it in `web/public/images/` (create the folder if it doesn't exist)
- [ ] Use it in the founder-story block of `web/app/(marketing)/page.tsx` (see TODO.md Week 1 Issue #4)

## Final quality gate (ask yourself these 5 questions before uploading)

1. **Does this look like a real photo taken on a real day?** (Yes / No)
2. **Would a stranger who saw this and then saw me on a Threads video later think "yes, same guy"?** (Yes / No)
3. **Is there anything AI-generated in this image — face, background, lighting, anything?** (Yes = redo, No = OK)
4. **Does this look like it could be my LINE profile picture?** (Yes / No)
5. **Would I be willing to have this photo next to my real name on a page selling NT$1,980 courses to strangers?** (Yes / No)

You need **5 yes's**. Any no → reshoot.

---

## What to do if you hate the result

You will hate it. That's normal. Most founders hate their own photo the first time they see it. Here is the test:

**Show it to ONE person who knows you. Ask: "Does this look like me?"**

- If they say *"yes, that's you"* → ship it. Your discomfort is not a reliability signal; their recognition is.
- If they say *"it looks a bit off, you don't usually look like that"* → reshoot, follow expression coaching more carefully.

Do NOT ask: *"Is this a good photo?"* — that invites polish-hunting. Ask only: *"Does this look like me?"*

---

## One final reality check

The goal is not a beautiful photo. The goal is a **credible** one. In the Taiwan IPAS prep market, credibility beats polish by 10×. Every minute spent past 15 minutes is a minute working against you — you're polishing past the point of believability and back toward the stock-photo look you started trying to avoid.
