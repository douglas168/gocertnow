An entrepreneur named Cories used a highly methodical, AI-driven approach to sell Pilates non-slip socks, generating $2.7 million in sales in just 30 days. His "zero to hero" journey can be broken down into several strategic steps from the sources:

**Step 1: Identifying a High-Growth Market**
Instead of entering a stagnant market where sellers only face competition and losses, Cories used AI and Google Trends to find a growing "blue ocean". He discovered the Pilates market, which had an annual growth rate of 11.5% and a consistently rising search volume.

**Step 2: Selecting the Right Product Using AI**
Rather than manually watching videos to find product ideas, he downloaded the subtitles from popular YouTube videos about "a day in the life of Pilates" and fed them directly to ChatGPT. He asked the AI to list all the products mentioned, which revealed a high-potential item: **Pilates non-slip socks**. A quick check on Google Trends confirmed that search interest for this specific item was also climbing.

**Step 3: Capitalizing on a "Brand Vacuum"**
Cories noticed a crucial detail in how his target audience talked about the product: they frequently expressed a need for non-slip socks, but never mentioned a specific brand. This indicated a **"brand vacuum"**—a market where consumers have a clear intent to buy, but no dominant brand has captured their loyalty, making it the perfect opportunity for whoever enters the space first.

**Step 4: Designing the Brand to Match User Identity**
Cories went a step further and used ChatGPT to analyze the video transcripts and extract identity tags for his target users. The AI identified the ideal customer as an exquisite, self-disciplined, and healthy girl who insists on fitness. When he asked ChatGPT which brand's vibe fit this aesthetic, it suggested "Gross" (likely a transcription of a popular brand). Cories **extracted its visual style—dreamy cloud backgrounds and soft pink tones—and applied it to his own brand's design and website**, naming his brand "Goar date" to capture the feeling of stability.

**Step 5: AI-Assisted Production and Setup**
With the brand identity established, he went to Alibaba's international platform to find a supplier and place his orders. Almost the entire setup process, including generating product images and building the brand website, was created with the participation of AI.

**Step 6: Smart Marketing and Highly Targeted Ads**
Instead of blindly spending money on broad advertising, Cories first posted a short video that organically blew up, reaching 2.1 million views. He then **ran highly targeted ads strictly to users who had watched over 95% of that video**. This hyper-focused strategy resulted in a massive ad conversion rate of 7.19%.

Ultimately, Cories succeeded because he didn't just treat AI as a chat tool; he used it as a business partner to find a growing market, identify user needs, and drastically improve efficiency from product selection to ad targeting.
